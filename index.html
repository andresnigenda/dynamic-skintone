Moving up the ladder? How skin tone determines social mobility in Mexico

Inequity is often a multidimensional phenomenon that disproportionately affects some sets of population. In Mexico this is particularly true among people with different skin tones. Some research has shown how lightest-skinned Mexicans have, on average, 1.5 additional years of schooling and 53% more hourly earnings than the darkest-skinned ones. This project explores how educational attainment has changed between Mexicans aged 25-64 in 2016 and their parents for different demographic and socioeconomic breakdowns.

This project uses data from Mexico's National Institute of Statistics and Geography (INEGI) 2016 Module on Intergenerational Social Mobility (MMSI).
